# CyberSentinel AI — Investor Q&A Playbook

> Prepared answers for the most common investor questions.  
> Practice these out loud before any pitch meeting.

---

## ❓ "Why will this win?"

**Answer:**

> "Most tools focus on detection only. We combine detection, investigation, and response into one AI-driven system. Security teams get a complete autonomous SOC — not another alert dashboard."

---

## ❓ "What's your moat?"

**Answer:**

> "Our advantage is the deep integration of real-time streaming, AI analysis, and automated response into a single platform. Competitors bolt AI onto legacy tooling — we built AI-first from day one."

---

## ❓ "Who is your customer?"

**Answer:**

> "Mid-sized companies and security teams that can't afford large SOC operations — typically 10–200 person IT/security departments. They need enterprise-grade defense without enterprise-grade headcount."

---

## ❓ "Why now?"

**Answer:**

> "Three forces are converging: AI-driven attacks are increasing exponentially, cloud adoption creates larger attack surfaces, and ransomware payouts are funding more sophisticated adversaries. Companies need automation to keep up."

---

## ❓ "How do you get users?"

**Answer:**

> "Start with pilot deployments at design-partner companies, then expand via self-serve SaaS subscriptions and MSSP/reseller partnerships. Security teams talk to each other — referrals drive growth."

---

## ❓ "What's your go-to-market strategy?"

**Answer:**

> "Three channels: (1) product-led growth through free trials and the live demo, (2) direct outreach to CISOs and SOC managers, (3) channel partnerships with MSSPs who white-label or resell the platform."

---

## ❓ "How does the AI work? Is it just GPT?"

**Answer:**

> "No. Our AI layer includes purpose-built models for threat correlation, campaign clustering, and IOC analysis. We use streaming event processing for real-time rule evaluation and graph-based investigation. LLMs augment analyst workflows but don't replace the core detection engine."

---

## ❓ "What's your team?"

**Answer:**

> *(Customize)* "We combine deep cybersecurity domain expertise with full-stack engineering. [Mention relevant backgrounds, security certifications, prior companies.]"

---

## ❓ "How do you price?"

**Answer:**

> "SaaS subscriptions — Starter at $49/mo for small teams, Pro at $199/mo for mid-market, and custom Enterprise pricing for large deployments. Expansion revenue comes from additional data connectors, analysts seats, and premium threat intel feeds."

---

## ❓ "What traction do you have?"

**Answer:**

> "We have a fully working prototype — end-to-end SOC system with multi-tenant SaaS architecture, real-time streaming pipeline, AI analyst, automated playbooks, and a production-ready marketing site. We're now onboarding pilot customers."

---

## ❓ "What's the use of funding?"

**Answer:**

> "$250K–$1M pre-seed to:
> - Scale engineering (2–3 hires)
> - Onboard 5–10 design partners
> - Security certifications (SOC 2 audit, pen testing)
> - Go-to-market (content, events, partnerships)"

---

## ❓ "What are the risks?"

**Answer:**

> "The main risks are (1) long enterprise sales cycles, which we mitigate with a self-serve tier, and (2) competition from incumbents, which we address by being AI-native and faster to ship. We also maintain strict data isolation — tenant data is never used for cross-tenant training."

---

## 🎤 Demo Tips

When you demo, **don't explain everything.** Just show:

1. **Attack** → detected on the globe  
2. **AI** → analyzes and correlates  
3. **System** → responds automatically  

That's it. Let the product speak.
